The Zepbound Food List for 2026: What to Eat (and Skip) on Tirzepatide

June 7, 2026 · 6 min read · By Chris Hardaway
Photo via Pexels — Silviu Din

It's 7 p.m. You made dinner, sat down, took four bites, and you're done. Not "watching your portions" done — physically, completely done. That's Zepbound. Tirzepatide doesn't just dial appetite down; it shuts the kitchen early. And that creates a problem nobody warns you about: when you can only eat 800-1,200 calories a day, every single bite has a job to do.

Eat the wrong things in that tiny window and you get the worst of both worlds — nausea, constipation, muscle loss, and nutrient gaps. Eat the right things and the weight comes off while you keep your strength and feel decent doing it. Here's the food list that makes the difference.

How Zepbound Changes the Way You Need to Eat

Video: Can I eat my favorite foods on a GLP-1 medication? — Ohio State Wexner Medical Center (YouTube)

Zepbound (tirzepatide) works on both GLP-1 and GIP receptors. It slows stomach emptying, blunts hunger signals, and quiets food noise. The practical consequences for your plate:

  • Portions shrink dramatically. A "meal" might be 300-400 calories. Three big meals a day stops being realistic — most people land on 4-5 small ones.
  • Fat sits heavy. Greasy food in a slow-emptying stomach is the classic tirzepatide nausea trigger.
  • Protein becomes non-negotiable. A meaningful share of weight lost on GLP-1 medications can come from lean mass if protein intake is too low. You can't afford to spend your tiny appetite on crackers.
  • Fiber and fluids stop being optional. Constipation is one of the most commonly reported Zepbound side effects, and eating less food means less fiber and water by default.

The Zepbound Food List: What to Eat

Protein Anchors (build every meal around one)

Eggs and egg whites, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, grilled chicken and turkey, white fish and salmon, lean beef, shrimp, tofu and tempeh, edamame, and protein shakes for the days solid food feels like a chore. Aim for 15-30 grams of protein per eating occasion. If you only have room for four bites, make them protein bites first.

Fiber That Keeps Things Moving

Berries, pears, and apples; oatmeal and overnight oats; lentils, black beans, and chickpeas in small servings; roasted or steamed vegetables — zucchini, green beans, carrots, broccoli if your stomach tolerates it. Raw cruciferous vegetables can be gassy on a slow stomach, so cooked usually beats raw in the first months.

Hydration Helpers

Water first, but also bone broth, soup, herbal tea, and sugar-free electrolyte drinks. Thirst cues get quieter on tirzepatide along with hunger cues, and mild dehydration is behind a lot of the fatigue and headaches people blame on the medication itself.

Gentle Carbs for Energy

Sweet potatoes, rice in small portions, whole-grain toast, bananas. Carbs aren't the enemy here — they're just the thing you add after protein and produce are handled.

The Zepbound rule of thumb: protein first, plants second, everything else only if there's room. Your appetite is now a small budget — spend it like one.

Foods to Limit or Skip

Nothing is banned forever, but these are the most common trouble-makers, especially during dose-increase weeks:

  • Fried and greasy food — the number one nausea trigger reported by tirzepatide users. A slow-emptying stomach and a pile of fat do not get along.
  • Very large portions — the "clean your plate" instinct now ends in hours of discomfort. Stop at satisfied, not full.
  • Carbonated drinks — extra gas in a stomach that's already slow to empty means bloating and pressure.
  • Alcohol — hits harder on a near-empty stomach, adds empty calories, and can worsen reflux and nausea.
  • Ultra-processed snack foods — not because they're evil, but because they spend your tiny calorie budget on nothing. 400 calories of chips is a whole meal's worth of appetite gone.

A Sample Day of Eating on Zepbound

Here's what the list looks like in practice — roughly 1,100 calories and 100+ grams of protein:

  • Morning: Greek yogurt with a handful of berries and a spoon of chia seeds.
  • Midday: Two scrambled eggs with a slice of whole-grain toast.
  • Afternoon: Clear protein drink or a cheese stick with an apple.
  • Evening: Half a grilled chicken breast, roasted zucchini, small scoop of sweet potato.
  • If room: Cottage cheese before bed — casein protein works for you overnight.

Notice what's missing: heroics. No 40-ingredient meal prep, no superfoods. Just protein anchors, cooked produce, and fluids, repeated in small portions.

The Bottom Line

Zepbound decides how much you eat. You still decide what. Build every small meal around a protein anchor, keep fiber and fluids coming, go easy on grease and bubbles, and track it — because at 1,000 calories a day, guessing is how muscle loss and nutrient gaps sneak in. The medication is doing its job. This food list is yours.

FAQ

What foods should you eat on Zepbound?

Prioritize lean protein at every meal — eggs, Greek yogurt, chicken, fish, cottage cheese, tofu — because muscle loss is the biggest risk during rapid weight loss. Add fiber from vegetables, berries, oats, and beans to manage constipation, and keep hydration up with water, broth, and electrolyte drinks. Small, protein-first meals are easier to tolerate than three large ones.

What foods should you avoid on Zepbound?

Greasy, fried, and very high-fat foods are the most common nausea triggers on tirzepatide because they sit in a stomach that's already emptying slowly. Carbonated drinks, alcohol, and very large portions also commonly cause discomfort. You don't have to ban any food forever — most people do best limiting these during dose-increase weeks.

How much protein do you need on Zepbound?

Many clinicians suggest roughly 0.7 to 1.0 grams of protein per pound of lean body mass while losing weight on a GLP-1 medication, spread across the day in small servings of 15 to 30 grams per meal. Because Zepbound shrinks appetite dramatically, hitting that target usually requires making protein the first thing on your plate at every meal. Confirm your personal target with your clinician.
