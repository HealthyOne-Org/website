Two Things SkinnyRx Does That Most Competitors Don't

1. Tirzepatide and Semaglutide in Pill Form

Most U.S. compounded GLP-1 programs ship injectables — you mix a vial, draw with a syringe, and inject subcutaneously once a week. That's a non-starter for plenty of people. Needle phobia, travel, the simple ick factor — all real reasons someone might never start a treatment that would otherwise help.

SkinnyRx offers both medications as oral pills. That's not a minor product difference. For a meaningful slice of would-be patients, "pill instead of injection" is the difference between starting and not starting. Note that compounded oral GLP-1s are not FDA-approved and the absorption profile is different from injectables; talk to the prescribing clinician about expectations.

2. Pay for Tirzepatide or Semaglutide with Affirm

GLP-1 treatment is expensive. Even compounded versions run a few hundred dollars a month, and SkinnyRx's pricing is fair for the category but not the cheapest on the market — the value is in the pill form and the financing. Affirm is integrated directly into checkout, so you can split the cost of tirzepatide or semaglutide across 3, 6, or 12 monthly payments instead of paying the full amount upfront. That's not a small thing — for a lot of people, the upfront cost is the actual barrier, not the monthly cost.
