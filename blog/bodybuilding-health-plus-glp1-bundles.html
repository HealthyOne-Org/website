Bodybuilding.com — yes, the same brand you've probably seen in protein-powder ads for the last twenty years — launched a telehealth wing called Bodybuilding Health+. They're now offering compounded GLP-1 treatment alongside the rest of their fitness ecosystem. Here's how the offering actually works and where it fits.

What Bodybuilding Health+ Is

Bodybuilding Health+ is a telehealth program built around compounded Semaglutide, a GLP-1 receptor agonist that helps regulate blood sugar, blunt appetite, and support weight loss. The pitch is straightforward: same active ingredient class as the brand-name injectables, but delivered through their licensed-provider network with the Bodybuilding.com brand around it.

The thing that genuinely differentiates them is the bundle structure — they sell treatment in 1-month, 3-month, 6-month, and 12-month commitments at progressively better pricing. They also offer microdosing protocols, which most other compounded GLP-1 providers don't.

Photo via Pexels — Gupta Sahil

Two Things Worth Flagging

1. The Bundle Pricing Model

Most GLP-1 providers default to a monthly subscription. Bodybuilding Health+ lets you pay for 3, 6, or 12 months at a time, with the per-month cost dropping at each tier. If you're confident you want to be on the medication for the full duration of a clinical taper (typically 6-12 months for meaningful weight loss), pre-committing to a bundle is usually cheaper than month-to-month.

The flip side: GLP-1 isn't right for everyone, and some people don't tolerate the side effects in the first few weeks. Starting with the 1-month or 3-month option, confirming you tolerate the medication, then renewing into a longer bundle is the more conservative path.

2. Microdosing Protocols

Microdosing in this context means lower-than-standard therapeutic doses — designed for people who want the metabolic and appetite-regulation effects of Semaglutide without the heavier side-effect profile of full-dose protocols. The clinical evidence on microdosing GLP-1s is still emerging, but anecdotally a lot of people who quit standard protocols due to nausea or GI side effects tolerate microdoses fine.

This is more of a "longevity" / metabolic-health framing than a "lose 50 pounds" framing. If your goal is moderate weight management plus metabolic benefits without going through the standard onboarding curve, microdosing protocols are a reasonable middle ground. Discuss with the prescribing clinician — they'll decide if it's appropriate for you.

Who Bodybuilding Health+ Is Probably Right For

  • You're committed to a longer GLP-1 protocol and want bundle pricing.
  • You want to try microdosing rather than full therapeutic doses.
  • You already trust the Bodybuilding.com brand from the fitness side.
  • You want a metabolic-health framing, not just weight loss.

Who Bodybuilding Health+ Is Probably Not Right For

  • You want pill-form GLP-1. See SkinnyRx.
  • You want maximum clinical track record & review volume. See bmiMD.
  • You only want to commit one month at a time and prefer to never see "12-month bundle" pricing upfront.
  • You need brand-name Wegovy® / Zepbound®. All compounded here.

The Same Compounded-GLP-1 Caveat

Worth repeating because it's important: compounded Semaglutide and Tirzepatide are not FDA-approved. The FDA hasn't independently evaluated the safety, effectiveness, or quality of compounded versions. They're prescribed legally under shortage and clinical-judgment exemptions, by licensed providers, but the FDA-approval gap is real. Bodybuilding Health+ discloses this explicitly in their terms. Read those before you start.
