## Post 2: "Photo vs. Voice Meal Logging"

### X/Twitter
Photo logging vs. voice logging — which is more accurate for tracking nutrition?

We tested both. Here's the honest answer (it depends on what you're eating).

healthyoneapp.com/blog/photo-vs-voice-meal-logging

### Instagram Caption
Photo or voice — which is better for logging meals?

We tested both while building HealthyOne. Here's what we found:

Photo is faster (3-5 sec) and catches everything visible on your plate. But it misses hidden ingredients like butter, oil, and marinades.

Voice is slightly slower (5-8 sec) but catches those hidden details — "grilled chicken IN olive oil."

The real answer? Use both. Photo for the plate, voice for what the camera can't see.

HealthyOne gives you 4 ways to log: photo, voice, text, and barcode. Use whatever fits the moment.

#meallogging #nutritionapp #AInutrition #foodtracking #healthtech #calorietracker #voicelogging #photologging

## Post 3: "Track Nutrition Without Counting Calories"

### X/Twitter
You don't need to count calories to eat well.

You need:
- Awareness of what you're eating
- Insight into nutrient gaps
- Something simple enough to do every day

5 ways to track nutrition without the misery:

healthyoneapp.com/blog/track-nutrition-without-counting-calories

### Instagram Caption
Stop counting calories. Seriously.

Here are 5 ways to track nutrition that actually stick:

1. Let AI do the math (snap a photo, done)
2. Focus on patterns, not numbers
3. Use the plate method (half veggies, quarter protein, quarter carbs)
4. Track nutrients, not just calories (a donut and chicken breast are NOT the same at 300 cal)
5. Build in accountability that isn't a red number

A 300-calorie donut and a 300-calorie chicken breast are not the same thing. Stop treating them equally.
